# Further reading

> *External references, organised by chapter. Not exhaustive — the durable ones.*

The chapters cite their own references inline. This page is for the durable starting points — books, papers, and resources that won't age out within a year.

## Autonomous labs — foundations

- **Häse F, Roch LM, Aspuru-Guzik A.** *Next-generation experimentation with self-driving laboratories.* Trends Chem. 2019;1(3):282–291.
- **Stach E, et al.** *Autonomous experimentation systems for materials development: a community perspective.* Matter. 2021;4(9):2702–2726.
- **MacLeod BP, et al.** *Self-driving laboratory for accelerated discovery of thin-film materials.* Sci Adv. 2020;6(20):eaaz8867.
- **Burger B, et al.** *A mobile robotic chemist.* Nature. 2020;583:237–241.

## Bayesian optimisation

- **Garnett R.** *Bayesian Optimization.* Cambridge University Press, 2023. (Free online.)
- **Frazier PI.** *A tutorial on Bayesian optimization.* arXiv:1807.02811. 2018.
- **Snoek J, Larochelle H, Adams RP.** *Practical Bayesian optimization of machine learning algorithms.* NeurIPS. 2012.
- **BoTorch documentation** — botorch.org.

## Reinforcement learning

- **Sutton RS, Barto AG.** *Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction* (2nd ed.). MIT Press, 2018.
- **Levine S, et al.** *Offline reinforcement learning: tutorial, review, and perspectives on open problems.* arXiv:2005.01643. 2020.
- **Spinning Up in Deep RL** — openai.com (still the most pedagogical RL intro).

## LLMs and agents in science

- **AI4Science / Microsoft Research** lecture series on LLMs for scientific discovery.
- **Bran AM, et al.** *Augmenting large language models with chemistry tools.* Nat Mach Intell. 2024;6:525–535. (ChemCrow.)
- **Boiko DA, MacKnight R, Kline B, Gomes G.** *Autonomous chemical research with large language models.* Nature. 2023;624:570–578.
- **Lu J, et al.** *Towards LLM-driven scientific discovery: a survey.* arXiv:2406.05804. 2024.

## Biomedical NLP

- **Demner-Fushman D, Elhadad N, Friedman C.** *Natural language processing for the clinical text: applications and challenges.* IMIA Yearbook of Medical Informatics. 2018.
- **Wang Y, Wang L, Rastegar-Mojarad M, et al.** *Clinical information extraction applications: a literature review.* J Biomed Inform. 2018;77:34–49.
- **Lee J, et al.** *BioBERT: a pre-trained biomedical language representation model.* Bioinformatics. 2020;36(4):1234–1240.
- **Gu Y, et al.** *Domain-specific language model pretraining for biomedical NLP.* ACM Trans Comput Healthc. 2022.

## Knowledge graphs

- **Hogan A, et al.** *Knowledge graphs.* ACM Comput Surv. 2021;54(4).
- **Himmelstein DS, et al.** *Systematic integration of biomedical knowledge prioritizes drugs for repurposing.* eLife. 2017. (Hetionet.)
- **Bonner S, et al.** *A review of biomedical datasets relating to drug discovery: a knowledge graph perspective.* Brief Bioinform. 2022.
- **Lobentanzer S, et al.** *Democratizing knowledge representation with BioCypher.* Nat Biotechnol. 2023.

## Retrieval-augmented generation

- **Lewis P, et al.** *Retrieval-augmented generation for knowledge-intensive NLP tasks.* NeurIPS. 2020.
- **Gao Y, et al.** *Retrieval-augmented generation for large language models: a survey.* arXiv:2312.10997. 2023.
- **Xiong G, et al.** *Benchmarking retrieval-augmented generation for medicine.* arXiv:2402.13178. 2024. (MedRAG.)

## Systematic-review support

- **Marshall IJ, Wallace BC.** *Toward systematic review automation: a practical guide to using machine learning tools in research synthesis.* Syst Rev. 2019;8:163.
- **van de Schoot R, et al.** *An open source machine learning framework for efficient and transparent systematic reviews.* Nat Mach Intell. 2021;3:125–133. (ASReview.)
- **PRISMA 2020 statement** — prisma-statement.org.
- **Cochrane Handbook for Systematic Reviews of Interventions** — training.cochrane.org/handbook.

## Hypothesis generation

- **Swanson DR.** *Fish oil, Raynaud's syndrome, and undiscovered public knowledge.* Perspect Biol Med. 1986;30(1):7–18.
- **Smalheiser NR.** *Rediscovering Don Swanson: the past, present, and future of literature-based discovery.* J Data Inf Sci. 2017;2(4):43–64.
- **Sybrandt J, et al.** *MOLIERE: automatic biomedical hypothesis generation system.* KDD. 2017.

## Safety, governance, model risk

- **NIH Office of Science Policy** — DURC and P3CO oversight (osp.od.nih.gov).
- **U.S. FDA** — *Good Machine Learning Practice for Medical Device Development* (joint principles, 2021).
- **U.S. FDA** — guidance on AI/ML-based Software as a Medical Device.
- **WHO** — *Ethics and governance of artificial intelligence for health.* 2021.
- **EU Artificial Intelligence Act** (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689).
- **Mitchell M, et al.** *Model cards for model reporting.* FAccT. 2019.

## Engineering practice — beyond this handbook

- **NeuroStack data-engineering chapters** — phindagijimana.github.io/neuro_stack/data-engineering/. Particularly the medallion-architecture pattern and the testing pyramid.
- **Site Reliability Engineering** (Beyer, et al., O'Reilly) — the SRE book; chapter 4 (SLOs) and chapter 11 (alerting).
- **Designing Data-Intensive Applications** by Martin Kleppmann. Especially Part II (distributed systems) and Part III (derived data).
- **Software Engineering at Google** (Winters, Manshreck, Wright) — for the platform-team patterns in chapter 25 onward.

## Communities, conferences, and venues

- **NeurIPS / ICML / ICLR** — methods.
- **AAAI, IJCAI** — applied AI in science.
- **MICCAI, IPMI, MIDL** — medical imaging methods and applications.
- **AMIA Annual / Informatics Summit** — biomedical informatics; literature synthesis tooling shows up here.
- **ISMRM, OHBM** — neuroimaging; many of the imaging features used in autonomous-lab biomarker work originate here.
- **NeurIPS AI4Science workshops** — methods for autonomous experimentation.
- **Acceleration Consortium** (University of Toronto) — community around self-driving labs.
