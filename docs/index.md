# AutoLabs

> *Autonomous Labs and Literature Synthesis — accelerating science itself.*

Biomedical science produces more data and more papers than any human can process. Two distinct technical movements have grown up to address that:

1. **Autonomous labs** — AI plus robots running experiments in a closed loop.
2. **Literature synthesis** — AI reading, extracting, and connecting what is already known.

Either alone is useful. Together, they are how science speeds up: the literature pipeline narrows the search space, the lab tests what's left, the lab's results feed back into the literature, and the cycle continues.

This handbook is a layered reference for that combined practice — written for four reader levels (beginner → senior research engineer), with neuroimaging examples kept concrete throughout.

## Two halves, every level

Every level treats both halves:

- **Part A — Autonomous labs.** Closed-loop experimentation. AI planners. Lab robotics. Feedback loops.
- **Part B — Literature synthesis.** Biomedical NLP. Knowledge graphs. Hypothesis generation. Systematic-review support.

## A note on hype

This space is over-promised on the internet and under-delivered in real labs. The handbook tries to be calibrated:

- It explains what works today.
- It marks what is still research.
- It names the failure modes — model hallucination, robot mispipetting, knowledge graphs with garbage edges, reproducibility crises — so you know what to budget for.

If a chapter sounds too optimistic, read it again with the warnings.
